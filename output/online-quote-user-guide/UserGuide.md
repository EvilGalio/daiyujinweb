# Online Quote — User Guide

Get started at [mfg-solution.com/online-quote](https://mfg-solution.com/online-quote/)

The Online Quote tool gives you a fast, data-driven cost estimate for CNC parts. Upload a STEP file, pick your materials and finishes, and you'll have a reference price in seconds.

It's designed for early-stage decisions — comparing options, scoping a project, or just getting a feel for what things cost. For a binding quote, our engineers will review your exact requirements and drawings.

## 1. Upload your STEP file

Click **Choose STEP files** and pick one or more `.stp` or `.step` files from your computer. You can upload several at once if you're evaluating multiple parts.

![Choosing and uploading STEP files](images/01-upload-step-files.png)

The closer your model is to the production version, the more useful the estimate will be.

## 2. Wait for geometry analysis

Once uploaded, the system reads your file and prepares a preview. You'll see a progress indicator showing what's happening in real time. It usually takes just a few seconds.

![STEP analysis in progress](images/02-step-analysis-progress.png)

When the part is ready, you can move on to choosing materials and manufacturing options.

## 3. Choose your material

Start with a material family on the left — Aluminum Alloy, Stainless Steel, Engineering Plastic, and so on. Then pick a specific grade from the list on the right, or search by name.

![Selecting material category and grade](images/03-select-material.png)

Don't see what you need? If your project calls for a specialty grade, imported material, or certified stock, just [request a formal quote](https://mfg-solution.com/request-quote/) and our team will find the right option.

## 4. Set your manufacturing parameters

These choices directly shape your estimate:

- **Process** — How the part is made. CNC Machining is the default.
- **General Tolerance** — ISO 2768 grade. Tighter tolerances affect cost.
- **Postprocess** — Surface finish or treatment, like Anodizing or Bead Blasting.
- **Quantity** — How many parts you need.
- **Currency** — USD or EUR.

![Setting process, tolerance, finish, quantity, and currency](images/04-process-tolerance-finish-quantity-currency.png)

If your part has tighter requirements than what you see here — local precision zones, specific inspections, heat treatment, or assembly fits — a formal quote is the way to go. Our engineers can account for all of it.

## 5. Add your contact details (optional)

You can leave your name and email if you'd like us to follow up. This step is entirely optional.

![Filling in contact details](images/05-contact-details.png)

If you do share your email, we'll use it to send your estimate and reach out if we have questions about your requirements. We treat your drawings and data as confidential business information.

## 6. Preview your part

The right side of the page shows a visual preview of your part. Two views are available:

- **Static PNG** — A quick reference image, great for confirming you uploaded the right model.
- **3D View** — Rotate, zoom, and inspect the geometry from any angle.

![Interactive 3D part preview](images/06-3d-part-preview.png)

You'll also see the file name, bounding dimensions, and volume. If anything looks off — wrong orientation, unexpected size — just upload the correct file and try again.

## 7. Work with multiple parts

When you upload several files at once, a Parts list appears on the left. Click any part to switch to it, and set its materials, process, and finish independently from the rest.

![Managing multiple parts in a single session](images/07-multiple-parts-list.png)

Parts that have been estimated show their cost in the list. Parts that are still waiting for you just say Ready. It's a quick way to evaluate a small batch, a multi-part assembly, or a few design alternatives all in one place.

## 8. See your estimate

Click the calculate button and your reference price appears on the right. You'll see the unit cost, total for your quantity, and a breakdown of what you selected — material, process, finish, and tolerance.

![Reference estimate result](images/08-reference-estimate.png)

This number is meant for early planning and comparison. When you're ready to move forward — for procurement, project approval, or a customer quote — click **Request Formal Quote**. Our engineering team will review your exact material grade, tolerances, finishes, inspection needs, and lead time to give you a precise, binding price.

## A few tips

- Double-check your model's units and dimensions before uploading.
- Not sure about a material or finish? Start with the defaults. You can always refine later.
- For complex parts — thin walls, tight fits, cosmetic surfaces, or custom testing — a formal engineering review will give you the most accurate result.
- Uploading multiple parts at once saves time. Set each one up individually without leaving the page.
- Online estimates are reference values. Final pricing always goes through our engineering team.
